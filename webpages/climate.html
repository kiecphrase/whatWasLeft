Climate and Weather and Things like that

Climate

The climate of the world can be described as similar to the Great Lakes region with notable climate extremes.

Most mountain ranges have extreme height elevation differences, with the foothills being more of a medium range between the standard lands and mountains.

The world has 3 major continents with dozens of minor island chains. Most islands have not been explored.

Foods

Corn and Potatoes are staple vegetables. Corn was the first revived domesticated crop, and there is a lot of cultural significance surrounding Corn.

Meat is less significant in the standard diet, fish is common on the coasts, and wild game is common in the wooded settlements. Some animals have been "domesticated", but they are mainly used for other purposes and not meat. The only widely domesticated animal specifically for meat is the chikpea, a small green bird that is semi-flightless. The birds and the eggs are commonly consumed.

Grains are very significant in the common diet. By accident, a semi-arid type of grain cross-pollinated with a quickly sprouting grain to create a fairly drought resistant crop that can be harvested multiple times in the warm months.

Fruits have been significantly domesticated and managed. From them, a veriety of alcohols have been produced.


Culture and Society

At this point, most societies are single villages or One larger fortress with small villages surrounding them. There are many of these small villages.

These societies are dotted around the major continents with significant distance between them. However, trade is extremely common and some societies practice semi-nomadic traditions.

Most societies are quasi-agrarian, with a craftmen class and warrior class being also prevalent in most societies.

Govenrments are extremely varried. Democracies exist, but many societies are ruled monarchally or oligarchally, and some follow a "strong-man leads" system in which the chief is chief as long as noone can best them in some measured capacity.


Religion

Religion is both nearly omnipresent and nonexistent; there is no unifying organized religion, but there is a common belief that many things house spirits and "gods". These deities typically are specific to locations, and there can be many nearly identical deities.

Most people pray to some "god", generally one directly related to their profession. Ancestors are also prayed to for guidance and to watch over surviving members of families.

A vast majority of people are spiritual, and those that are not at least acknowledge that the spirits do exist but simply choose not to seek divine intervention from them.
